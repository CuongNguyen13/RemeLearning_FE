# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Self-directed English learners reviewing their own speaking recordings. They log in, check the
weak points and recommendations surfaced from their past recordings, then move into practice or
dictation flows to work on exactly those gaps. Success looks like fewer repeated mistakes over
time, not raw usage volume.

## Product Purpose

RemeLearning analyzes a learner's own recorded speech (transcription + diarization) to detect
recurring and forgetting-prone mistakes, then turns those into weak points and recommendations the
learner can act on directly inside the app (practice, dictation). It exists to replace generic
drill practice with feedback grounded in what the learner actually said.

## Positioning

Spaced repetition against your own mistakes: the app resurfaces exactly the weak points you're
about to forget, generated from your real recordings rather than a fixed curriculum.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, playful, motivating — a study companion that celebrates progress and keeps momentum up,
without tipping into the generic AI-SaaS dashboard look (no hero-metric tiles, no gradient text, no
side-stripe accent cards, no identical icon+heading+text grids repeated everywhere).

## Anti-references

Generic AI-SaaS dashboard cliché: hero-metric-card templates, gradient text, side-stripe borders,
repeated identical card grids, tiny uppercase eyebrows above every section.

## Design Principles

- Ground every recommendation in the learner's own data — surface *why* a weak point exists (which
  recording, how often, how recently) rather than presenting it as generic advice.
- Motivating, not clinical — celebrate progress and momentum energetically, but keep it credible;
  avoid childish gamification (mascots, confetti, badge-spam).
- One primary action per screen — recordings → weak points → recommendations → practice/dictation
  is a linear loop; each screen should make the next step in that loop obvious.
- Consistent visual language across the three analysis domains (vocabulary, grammar,
  pronunciation) — same weak-point/recommendation card shape, differentiated by icon/color per
  domain, not by structure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA across all screens; respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animation.
